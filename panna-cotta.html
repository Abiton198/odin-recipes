Panna-cotta

A traditional, easy, and delicious Italian custard. It can be very difficult and not so easy to find one on the internet, so you shoukd be prepared to make your own recipe for your own test. It tastes just like the panna conna served at Italian restaurants.

Panna-cotta can be served with warm hot fudge sauce and fresh raspberries on top. This keeps well for several days in the refrigerator.

Ingredients;

  • 1/3 cup skim milk
      • 1(.25 ounce) envelope unflavored gelatin
  • 2 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons vanilla extract

Steps:

  1. Pour milk into a small bowl, and stir in the gelatin powder. Set aside.
  2. In a saucepan, stir together the heavy cream and sugar, and set over medium heat. Bring to full boil, watching, carefully, as the cream, will quickly rise to the top of the pan.
  3. Pour the gelatin and milk into milk into the cream, stirring until completely dissolved.
  4. Cook for one minute, stirring constantly. Remove from heat, stir in the vanilla and pour into six individual ramekin dishes.
  5. Cool the ramekins uncovered at room temperature. When cool, cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, but preferably overnight before serving.
